Franz Josef, South Island, New Zealand
Sunday, March 2, 2014

After a refill at the local supermarket for food and petrol I looked in the Hokitika jade shops. Lunch was on the shore of Lake Wahapo which provided a wonderful view. 

There is so little traffic on the roads that you go for miles before you have someone behind you. Every so often you pass through a few houses and the odd store but these are quickly left behind. There are regular small areas of roadworks which amuse us. The men wave to you and only occasionally stop the traffic. There's no miles and miles of single lane traffic you just drive in the unmade road while they work around you. So unlike UK highway maintenance.

We are camped in a rainforest campsite right in the centre of Franz Joseph village which is at the foot of a major glacier, currently invisible to us as it's raining. This afternoon we went to see the Kiwi Wildlife conservation centre which works in partnership with the Department of Conservation to increase the number of Rowi kiwis in NZ. There are only around 350 in the world. Our visit included the hatchery and incubation area. They currently have two kiwi eggs awaiting hatching and one 21 day old baby in the incubator. The pens also have three kiwis that can be seen scourging in the undergrowth of their simulated nighttime habitat as of course they are nocturnal.

We are really hoping the rain will stop tomorrow so we can walk to see views of the Franz Josef glacier. Forecast looks good so let's keep our fingers crossed.

Where I stayed
