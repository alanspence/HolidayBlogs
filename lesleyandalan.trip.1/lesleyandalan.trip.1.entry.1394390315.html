Lake Tekapo, South Island, New Zealand
Sunday, March 9, 2014

We travelled through gorges and mountains with pleats, folded like a sheet of material, miles of bare countryside with no settlements and only scrub, to the Mount Cook National Park. We picnicked looking at the snow on Mount Cook range which offered a wonderful vista for our break. 

We are now in the prime camping spot overlooking Lake Tepako which we have now worked out can be achieved by booking ahead using the free phone number they supply. 

We walked along the lake into Tepako village to top up our milk supply returning as we felt it was time to get out our chairs again to soak up the view and the suns rays while watching the water skiers and brave swimmers in the cold water. The lake is fed from a glacier! This and the lake we drive beside to Mount Cook village are amazingly blue in colour as they're full of ground rock particles called 'flour' made by the glaciers, and these cause the intense colour.

Our view and spot here is so good we've decided to stay a second night, our last one in the van, as we'd like to finish at a lovely place and at a slower pace.

Where I stayed

Lake Tekapo Motels & Holiday Park - 2 Lakeside Drive, Lake Tekapo, New Zealand
