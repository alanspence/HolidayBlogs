Springfield, South Island, New Zealand
Friday, February 28, 2014

Camping across the beach from the whale watching tour office proved to be an excellent choice of camp site, must admit it was a recommendation from Michelle and Andrew!! You are taken to the marina by coach after watching safety briefings and warnings about sea sickness. I decided to take a tablet to prevent me being distracted from the whales by feeling ill, shame about half the boat didn't too!

About half way through the trip I started to feel disgusting together with several other guests. I am informed that Flyer bounces through the waves even more, which decided me about not joining Robin's crew on a rough day.  Alan didn't feel quesy at all.  Alan was delighted to have a lady immediately to his left sick several times, me to his right looking like death and being sick once, and a lady one seat behind also sick.  They have a person on board described as medical officer but actually she's a sick bag collector and hander out of tissues, wipes, and water. 
 
You remain seated inside the jet propelled catamaran boat as the boat travels to the whale spotting areas while listening to a great commentary by a knowledgable guide. We saw three whale sightings and the boat went really near the whales each time. The first spot saw the whale showing it's teeth and eating a fish which the guide was very excited about.  We were close to the whale for several minutes, seeing it spout as it breathed through its blowhole, before it dived below again, and the boat went off at high speed to try for another sighting. It was a wonderful experience.
 
We were so lucky that our trip went ahead as the two later ones were cancelled due to the poor weather forecast for later, as it would have been dangerous to take the boat out. It certainly is not an experience I would have wanted to miss.

Whilst driving south east towards the start of Arthur's Pass we ran into torrential rain which fell as snow over the mountains we were approaching. The camp site had magnificent views of the snow capped mountains although at £3.75 per person a night you couldn't moan about the spartan facilities.

Where I stayed

Kaikoura Top 10 Holiday Park - 34 Beach Road, Kaikoura, New Zealand

What I saw

Kaikoura Whale Watching tour - Whaleway station, Kaikoura, Christchurch-Canterbury, South Island, New Zealand
