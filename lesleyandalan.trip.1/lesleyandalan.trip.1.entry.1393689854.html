Hokitika, South Island, New Zealand
Saturday, March 1, 2014

Leaving the flat valley floor behind and comments that there was snow in the pass we drove across the pass. Initially the cloud was low which excluded some views but this quickly disappeared to reveal wonderful views of the mountain ranges. 
 
Stopped at Arthur's Pass village and walked to the Devils Punchbowl waterfall. Due to last night's rain it was fairly full which made the steep climb up to it worthwhile.

We had a picnic lunch at a viewpoint in the pass, but had to keep our windows and doors closed because of the Kea parrots which are very curious birds and tried to come inside the camper van to eat our food. The went inside the driver's cabin of a worker's van which parked nearby!

The pass is not hazardous as long as you drive fairly slowly on the twisty bends and in fact much of it is fairly flat along wide river valleys which are almost dry after the summer months. Of course here Autumn is just commencing and the sheep will be brought down from the summer pastures soon.

We're now at Hokitika which is the town featured in the latest Booker prize winner, The Luminaires which I (Alan) read recently.  It was a gold rush town and a fascinating harbour entrance where many ships foundered in 1865 and other years around then, at one stage a ship was wrecked every 10 weeks, which makes running aground around Brightlingsea seem tame. It was interesting to compare the town to the vivid images in the novel.

We went for a long walk along the beach to the town and then when it was dark visited the glow worm dell opposite the camp site. We saw many glow worms, but to be honest Christmas fairy lights are better and flash.  LEDs 1 Glow worms 0.

Camping by the beach is lovely as you can hear the waves as you fall asleep. 

Where I stayed

Hokitika Kiwi Holiday Park - 242 Stafford Street, Hokitika, New Zealand
