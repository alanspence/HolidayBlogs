Christchurch, South Island, New Zealand
Wednesday, March 12, 2014

We headed out of the area of devestation, dodging diggers, fluorescent jacketed workmen and endless wire fenced off areas towards the west edge of the main area today. 

Enjoyed looking around the Museum which has some very well presented and varied displays and also has art displays as the Art Museum was damaged during the earthquake and is still undergoing renovations. There was an excellent exhibition of Street Art and work by Banksy.

We then headed into the Botanic Gardens which were still looking beautiful in the sunlight although there were noticeably more leaves turning now as Autumn's here.  It was refreshing to see part of the city mostly unaffected by 2010/2011.

There are hardly any, and we really mean any shops left in the city centre so we headed to a mall a little way out, just to see where everyone is and get a sight of everyday Christchurch. It was buzzing with people and again good to see normality after the devastation of the central city.

Back to the hotel then to start packing!  So excited to be getting back to the kids as it feels so long since we have seen them ........... But we have had the most amazing holiday ever.  

Anyone wondering whether to do a similar trip to this side of the world, don't hesitate, DO IT. 

 

Where I stayed

Rendezvous Hotel Christchurch - 166 Gloucester Street, Christchurch, New Zealand

What I saw
