Auckland, North Island, New Zealand
Tuesday, February 18, 2014

Auckland is built on 50 volcanos but the last one to erupt was 600 years ago, let's hope it stays that way too. Some of Auckland's volcanos are cones, some are filled with water and some completely quarried away and NZ is trying to get them known as a World Heritage site in order to protect them.

Our walk took us to some of the less known places which included the replica of Michelangelo's Moses statue in Myers Park. Passing by the Town Hall, and Michelle and Andrews hotel !, we then did a tour of the Auckland Art gallery which gave an insight into the local art. Beautiful building extension which opened in 2011 after a $121 million refurbishment. The glass and wood atrium is light and airy, reflecting the colours and movement of the trees in Albert Park through the gaps and windows.
 
The university is housed partially in Government House which was New Zealand's seat of Power before Wellington became the capital. Auckland is said to be the fastest growing city since the start of the 20th century. There continues to be a lot of redevelopment around the harbour area, which was commenced in preparation for the Rugby World Tour in 2011.

We continued to the Wynyard Quarter, once a busy commercial port which now hosts many dining areas, waterfront cafés and marinas. The area includes the America Cup boatyard and many moorings for impressive yachts, well Alan was impressed anyway.  Although tempted by joining a trip on the America Cup boat we declined as time was at a premium and the total sailing time was not very long. I decided I should probably start on Flyer anyway rather than the America cup boat! We did see the packed boat of tourists return to the harbour though.
 
 Last night in Auckland before returning to the airport area to pick up the camper van.
