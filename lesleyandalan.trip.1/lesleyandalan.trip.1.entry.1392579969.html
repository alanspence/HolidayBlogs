Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
Sunday, February 16, 2014

Caught the free tram to the east of the city, to Parliament Building where we saw the Treasury Building including its gold vaults which they used during the gold rush. The Aussie guide took us on a personal tour to see the Governor's chair and Queen's chair (which she apparently has never used) in the conference room. She liked the fact we were English and so gave us a private tour of the upstairs rooms which aren't open to the public!

Then into the city to China Town for a meal before heading to Fitzroy Gardens to see the Fairy Tree (thanks Jo for telling us about it) and Captain Cook's house. Beautifully kept gardens minutes from the hubbub of the centre of the City. 

We passed the huge Melbourne Cricket Ground before heading to the Olympic site where they were holding a Bruce Springsteen concert in AAMI Park. Did try and get tickets for tonight but the only ones available were at the very back of the stadium and had a restricted view. Unfortunately we weren't previously aware he was playing. 

Across the road to see the Rod Laver Arena where the open tennis was played a few weeks ago in 45 degrees heat. Thank goodness it's not that hot today. Oh and the flies have gone, thanks to rain in the morning and stronger winds blowing them back towards the deserts they belong in! Alan was relieved to see that Lional Richie wasn't playing till March so he had to miss that experience.
 
We walked back to our hotel along the River Yarra to start packing. New Zealand tomorrow.

What I saw

City Museum at Old Treasury - Spring St, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) - Yarra Park, Jolimont, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
