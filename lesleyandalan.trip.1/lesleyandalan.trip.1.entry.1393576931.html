Kaikoura, South Island, New Zealand
Friday, February 28, 2014

The three hour ferry crossing was booked for 9am so that we could make the most of the day. Wellington harbour is a huge natural harbour and the ferry takes a while to navigate it. I was constantly told about all the different coloured buoys and why we taking the chosen course! The Cook Straight was very calm and was over by the time the coffee was drunk. Back on the observation deck we were able to enjoy the views of the spectacular Queen Charlotte Sound which takes about an hour before you arrive in South Island at Picton.

Leaving Picton behind we drove straight to Blenheim wine country. The growing area is irrigated by the Wairau River and sheltered by the forested Richmond Range to the north and the bare slopes of the Kaikoura mountains to the south.

We visited several vineyards including Cloudy Bay winery and of course bought samples! 

Leaving the sunny valley floor behind we travelled along the magnificent coast road enjoying spectacular endless sands and ocean to Kaikoura ready for whale watching tomorrow. 

Where I stayed
