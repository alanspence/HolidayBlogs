Wanaka, South Island, New Zealand
Tuesday, March 4, 2014

Travelled further along the east coast of South Island along the coast road, Well it's the only road!! We laughed when the Sat Nav said 'Turn Right in 235 kms!'. Very little traffic on the roads, mainly camper vans. You drive for literally kms and kms without seeing any settlements at all, not even a farm, just beautiful countryside. Today was one of the best drives, out of the glacier area, into rainforest again, onwards towards snow topped mountains in the distance, along massive glacial river valleys, over a pass through the mountains, and then out to drive along two beautiful lakes with blue water surrounded on all sides by the loveliest mountains.

We arrived at our site at the southern end of Lake Wanaka, and parked up and had a cup of tea before driving the short distance back into the town. We walked along the lakeside which has been delightfully landscaped and is surrounded by stunning hills and mountains. We looked in a few gift shops and explored before enjoying an excellent Indian meal and sampling the local beer.

This town feels the biggest we've seen for a few days, but that's only because it's got a central area about three blocks by three instead of just one main road.

We also met today some new friends as lovely as the flies in Melbourne, the famous southern midges and sand flies. One gave me (Alan) a sharp bite, no doubt we'll be flicking them off for the next few days!

Where I stayed

Aspiring Holiday Park & Motels - Studholme Road North, Wanaka, New Zealand
