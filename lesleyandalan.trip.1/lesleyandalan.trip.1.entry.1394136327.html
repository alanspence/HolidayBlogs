Te Anau, Southland, New Zealand
Thursday, March 6, 2014

Before leaving Queenstown we walked around the lake edge watching the steam ship and the jet boat leaving the marina.

Te Anau took about 2.5 hours to reach as it's 170 km south west of Queenstown and serves as a service town at the entrance to the fiords.

After arriving, we walked into the town, around yet another beautiful lake edge, and looked at the shops. We had a hokey pokey ice cream, apparently only available in New Zealand. Our camp site has wonderful views of the lakeside and we have decided it is probably one of our favourite sites so far. We are enjoying relaxing and just looking at the view.

Tomorrow we are taking a coach into the fiord area of Milford Sound. It's a five hour return trip along a narrow windy road, plus a two hour boat ride and also it's Alan's birthday so we thought it would be more relaxing not doing any driving.
