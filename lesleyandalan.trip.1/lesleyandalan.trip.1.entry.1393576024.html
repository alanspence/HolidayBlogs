Wellington, North Island, New Zealand
Thursday, February 27, 2014

Our campsite was very convenient and served as an excellent city car parking space to see Wellington city. We bumped into Ruth and  Christina along the waterfront, they had been dropped off by Ruth's parents who they are staying with for a weeks holiday. Very strange seeing relatives out of context but lovely to meet up.

We took  the cable car to the top of the hill so we could see the view of the harbour and city itself. Wellington has a huge natural harbour and the view was worth the ride. Richard and Ruth went to university in Wellington so it was good to see where they spent that part of their lives.

The Botanic Gardens were impressive, much nicer than Sydney's gardens near the Opera house. These were full of colour and had such varied areas of planting.

The Te Papa Museum is also impressive and will be remembers for the displays but also the fast internet speed and unlimited coffee refills! 

In the evening we were joined by Ruth's brothers and parents for a meal in a pub five minutes from our camp site. 

What I saw

Museum of New Zealand (Te Papa Tongarewa) - Cable St, Wellington, North Island, New Zealand
Wellington Botanic Garden - Tinakori Rd, Wellington, North Island, New Zealand
Wellington Cable Car - Lambton Quay, Wellington, North Island, New Zealand
