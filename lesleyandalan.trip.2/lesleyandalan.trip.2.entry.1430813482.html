Xi'an and the Terracotta Warriors

Xi'an, Shaanxi, China
Tuesday, May 5, 2015

The Impressive gardens and Buddah temple at the Big Wild Goose Pagoda in Xi'an provide an area of tranquility in this bustling city centre.

The Terracotta Warriors are located at the edge of a hill/mountain range towards the East of Xi'an. These remnants are from 1000BC to 1000AD and were constructed to guard Qin Shi Haung's tomb over 2000 years ago. The farmer Yang Zhifa, who discovered the initial terracotta fragments in 1974, when drilling a well was at the excavation signing guide books today!

Three rectangular pits have been excavated and are at varying stages of completion. In the last few days the dig in pit two has restarted due to advanced technology and resources being available.

The brick floors and wooden supports, many with burnt ashes where invaders tried to destroy the figures, are clearly seen over the ranks of soldiers. In pit 1 2000 figures were discovered with an estimated 4000 left to be discovered. Figures average 1.8 m in height and are hollow inside from the thigh up. Every figure of different ranks have different features with their hair tied up in a bun. The pits have five sloping entrances on the four rectangular sides, used for constructing the pit in the Qin period. Once the pit construction was completed the entrances were sealed with wooden figures.

Pit two and three are smaller and are thought to contain even more warriors than pit one but this is yet to be known. The whole figures in the photos are from pit two. It was in pit two that the only complete figures found during the excavations are 160 kneeling archers holding crossbows, the others have all been restored. Many figures in different stages of restoration are seen.

Pit three was discovered in 1976 and so far only 68 pottery figures have been found.

Nearby are the Huaqing Hot Springs which have mineral riches water which have emerged at 43 degrees for 2500 years. It has a complex of restored bathing houses and pools which was created in the Tang Dynasty.
