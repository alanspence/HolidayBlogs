Chongqing, Chongqing, China
Sunday, May 10, 2015

Woke early to see we were already docked at Chongqing,the largest city in China, with 33 million inhabitants. The porters carried our luggage up the 100 steps to the awaiting minivan, for a tip of course!

Visited the old city area which has been redeveloped by a local woman who wanted to maintain the old culture so persuaded the Government to retain the old building design. Across the river the guide told us was just the river bank eight years ago but today it is a mass of sky scrapers, the new opera house, shaped like a boat to reflect the importance of the convergence of the two rivers. The bridge behind it mirrors the Sydney Bridge and another building is shaped like the Chrysler Building in USA.

Flew from Chongqing to Guillin, just over a one hour flight.

From the airport went straight to the Reed Flute Cave in Guilin. The area is all limestone which is why the hills are all rounded and there many caves. These caves were only found in 1958 by children playing.Guillin is famous for its landscapes of craggy 200m hills on the Li River.

Fubo Hill is a rounded hill in the centre of the city and alongside the river. People were swimming in the river, definitely didn't fancy that!
