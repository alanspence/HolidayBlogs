Shanghai, Shanghai Shi, China
Tuesday, April 28, 2015

No rice or noodles chosen by us for breakfast ! Sausage, bacon and fried egg instead.

Lightening visit of the impressive Shanghai Museum, the shape of the building is designed to replicate a ding, an ancient Chinese bronze vessel. Exhibits range from bronzes, stone carvings, jadeware and porcelain from the Ming and Qing eras.

Crossed the city to Shanghai's holiest Jade Buddha Temple, festooned with rich red lanterns and halls and temples of dark wood framing the Buddha statues. The highlight is a 1.9m pale green Jade Buddha.

Shanghai is noted for its silk and tailored clothing and the need to transport the tourist to associated shops! Demonstration of how silk duvets are made and of course the opportunity to buy any size and weight duvet, beautiful quilt covers, shirts, jackets and scarfs.

Lunch was soup dumplings and more dumplings with a variety of fillings.

Back into the car which appears like magic ready to transport us to our next destination, Yuyuan Gardens. Classic tranquil gardens comprise of four main elements, bonsai, water, architecture and rock. These 'mountains' were transported 500 km to make the garden complete.
The Old Town, is dark wooden buildings and are a mecca of souvenir shops. We are getting used to ignoring the many people selling selfie sticks and wheels for your shoes, we don't need them we have a driver!!

Quick ride on the sightseeing tram under the Huangpu River to the Pudong business district, created from the flatland of boggy farms in the last 20 years. You may remember the Frenchman who scaled the not yet opened 128 floor Shanghai Tower without ropes in February.

50 minute turn around before dinner and the Acrobatic Show at the theatre. The climax of the show were eight motorcyclists rotating inside a metal cage, quite amazing and certainly we haven't seen anything like it before.
