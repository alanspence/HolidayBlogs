Beijing, Shanghai, China
Friday, May 1, 2015

Leisurely visit to the Temple of Heaven in central Beijing where the emperors of the Ming and Qing Dynasties held ceremonies to worship heaven and pray for a good harvest. First built in 1420 Ming era it is the largest and best preserved sacrificial architecture in the world. The temple covers an area of 273 hectares.
When viewed from above the temples are round and their bases square, the pattern reflecting the Chinese view that heaven is round and the earth square.

The Hall of Prayer for good harvests is mounted on a three tiered marble terrace and decorated in stunning blue, yellow and green glazed tiles.

In the grounds many elderly people were singing, playing board (mah jong) and ball games and exercising.

The driver then took us to the traditional alleys of the Hutong area of the city which is where both the poor and relatives of the Emperor lived. We got on a rickshaw which took us along the narrow scruffy streets and to a family's courtyard home - all part of the tourist trail which the Government has recognised the potential of!

Plenty of dumplings and Chinese specialities for dinner at a local restaurant.

Next to Beijing Zoo where we had excellent views of five pandas including 18 month old twins. The guide was delighted with how awake they all were as they are often inactive.

Last stop the Olympic village to see modern architecture of the Birds Nest and Water Cube. The Birds Nest National Stadium was designed by the Swedish Architectural firm Herzog and De Meuron. Hope Leon you are impressed at my knowledge! The Olympics brought much wealth and change to Beijing.
