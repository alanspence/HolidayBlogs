Beijing, Shanghai, China
Thursday, April 30, 2015

6am breakfast ready to catch the Bullet Train. The station is 1/2 an hour with no traffic from the city centre and on the opposite side of Beijing from the International Airport which has apparently affected it's passenger numbers. The Bullet Train travels between Shanghai and Beijing at 300 km/h (186 mph) on the high-speed line in as little as 4 hours 48 minutes. The carriage display showed it regularly travelling at 303 km/hr. Bullet trains were first introduced into China in 2008, and since then, China's rail witnessed a boom of this fast way of transportation across the country. Lots of people were travelling as it is Labour Day tomorrow and residents have a week's holiday.

We were free to entertain ourselves for the rest of the day so we walked along the shopping street, again full of well known names. Tiffany's, Apple, H and M to name a few. The massive street market sold everything, however I wasn't tempted by the BBQ locusts, live scorpion type creatures on a stick, Starfish again on a stick and many more culinary delights.

The Tiananmen Square and Forbidden City area was very crowded due to the Labour Day holiday. Random spot street checks were seen to be made by the many Policemen and Military staff on several younger tourists walking near Tiananmen Square. The Military personnel looked stick thin!!, never smiled and firmly gripped their batons. No attempt was made to speak to them or take photos for sure. We certainly under estimated the size of the Forbidden City as the perimeter seemed to go on for ages when circumnavigating it to rush back to meet the other people from our group to go out for dinner. At 18.00 the temperature was still 30 degrees but apparently rain is expected tomorrow when the guide will take us inside the Forbidden City and onto the Square itself.
