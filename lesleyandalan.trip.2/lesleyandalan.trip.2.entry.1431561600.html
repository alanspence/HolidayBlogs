Hong Kong, Guangxi Zhuang, China
Thursday, May 14, 2015

We took the hotel bus to Kowloon Station and then the subway to the base of the cable car on Lantau Island. Just north of Lantau Island is HK airport which was moved from near our hotel in the east to its location on the new man made Island. We could see the building of another Island and bridge structure on the cable car but don't know what it will become.

The bronze Buddha on top of the mountain facing the monastery, was completed in 1993 and stands 26m tall, reached by 260 steps. It is the tallest seated Buddha in the world was made from 220 bronze pieces and sits on a throne of lotus, the Buddhist symbol of purity. The Po Lin Monastery is regarded as a important temple for the area, becoming an important place for pilgrimage in the 1920's.

In the evening we ventured to Temple Street Night Market.
