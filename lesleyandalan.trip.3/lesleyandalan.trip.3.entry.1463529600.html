Farmington, New Mexico, United States
Wednesday, May 18, 2016

Reluctantly left Santa Fe behind as there was still so much to do and climbed higher onto the mountain desert road on the land belonging to the Pueblo Indians. Interestingly we were told that the Indians are not governed by the Federal Government and any negotiations are done via the FBI not the local Police as State Law does not apply to Indians on reservations.

The scenery was ever changing, the scrubby high desert vegetation and incredible land masses that can't be captured very well in a bumpy car on a photo.

Mountains were left behind and the long straight road passed over the Continental Divide and disappeared into the wilderness ahead before surprising us by seeing more chemical and oil plants.

We stopped for a break and had coffee - the shop next door sold "guns, ammo, gifts". We went in to have a look, and there were several second hand saddles as well as plenty of guns on show.

Four corners is the place where four states meet, Utah, Colorado, Arizona and New Mexico a few miles from where we stopped for the night.

Where I stayed

Comfort Inn - 555 Scott Ave, Farmington, United States
