Bryce, Utah, United States
Sunday, May 22, 2016

It was a very enjoyable four hour drive to Bryce Canyon at 7,956'. Rather than a canyon it is more of an amphitheatre eroded from the cliffs. It has sorbet coloured pinnacles and points, steeples and spires and totem pole shaped hoodoo's. We walked down to the floor of the amphitheatre and back up of course,which gave us a different perspective of the Hoodoo's. It was hot in the afternoon but with the overnight temperature forecast to be -5C there was a distinct cold feel to the air when we went for a sunset walk and got back to our lodge in the park at 9pm.
