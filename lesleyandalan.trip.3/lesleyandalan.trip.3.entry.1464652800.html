Mammoth Lakes, California, United States
Monday, May 30, 2016

We stopped to look at the sand dunes at the Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes which were draped in the morning sun, beautiful. There was a long incline out of Death Valley towards the Sierra Nevada mountains for our last leg of our journey towards San Francisco and Santa Rosa. Leaving Death Valley but still in the desert we drove north along Owens Valley with craggy snow capped mountains on both sides of the valley, passing the highest mountain in California, Mt Whitney at 14,505'.

We passed by the Manzanar Historic Site where in 1942, the United States government ordered more than 110,000 men, women, and children to leave their homes and detained them in remote, military-style camps. Manzanar War Relocation Center was one of ten camps where Japanese American citizens and resident Japanese were interned during World War II.

The occasional town we passed all had USA flags on display at regular intervals along the pavement as it is Memorial Day. They certainly are more proud of veterans than the UK with many tourist attractions offering free admission to them.

Another long incline brought us to Mammoth Lakes an all year round resort, skiing, walking and a huge area for mountain biking. The snow is still present and consequently the Devils Postpile National Monument, 60' pillars formed from lava from a volcanic eruption which cooled and fractured into columns is shut due to it being too early in the season. It's noticeable how the trees are just in bud or leaf which is such a contrast to middle USA areas of The Mississippi.

We are carefully watching the National Park Service site for more snow news as the ranger said the weather is still unpredictable in this area and we need to know whether we can take the much shorter but very high pass into Yosemite, or go a much longer route in.
