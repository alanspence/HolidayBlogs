Santa Fe, New Mexico, United States
Tuesday, May 17, 2016

Started at the farmers market before heading towards the 400 year old Plaza in the heart of downtown where the Indian and Spanish markets are held. This was where we were advised we to get genuine Indian wares rather than imports from Pakistan and Mexico. The Pueblo Indians sit under the covered walkway of the Palace Of Governors which dates back to 1610, all year round to make a living. Talking to them was fascinating, they travel for 3 hours to sell the pottery and jewellery that they have made.

We sat in the Plaza in the sun listening to the musicians and eating lunch. The weather forecast predicted rain today but none materialised but there is a noticeable chill in the desert air today. Snow can be seen on the nearly mountain tops.

We visited the Georgia O'Keeffe museum. She is a major USA artist who lived in her later life in the desert mountains north of Santa Fe. We enjoyed her works in the exhibition and may visit her ranch on our journey tomorrow to the north of the town.

We passed by the Loretto Chapel, a Gothic-Revival style chapel was completed in 1878. We didn't go inside but it's famous for the chapel's Miraculous Staircase which has two 360-degree turns and no apparent means of support. It's privately owned with an admission charge for what we decided was a marketing gimmick - anything "Miraculous" with a charge is suspect!

The Cathedral Basilica of St Francis of Assisi was simple in design but attractive.

There are so many art galleries it must be a record, in fact they have a whole road dedicated to them as well as being in the town centre. Many of the shops have jewellery made from the local mined turquoise, the gemstone of this Southwest area but it's hard to tell which is genuine to the untrained eye so I wasn't tempted. Turquoise represents origins and belonging, protection and health, abundance and beauty. It stands for all aspects of a good life and symbolises water, sky, rain, sun, bountiful crops and healthy, happy children.

Picked up a few provisions and walked back to our Adobe B and B. Lots more to do tomorrow.
