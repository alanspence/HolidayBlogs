Savannah, Georgia, United States
Saturday, April 30, 2016

Stopped at Beaufort on the way to our destination of Savannah. This Lowcountry region had been subject to numerous European explorations and several aborted attempts at colonization before the British successfully founded the city in 1711.

The Gullah are known for preserving more of their African linguistic and cultural heritage than any other African-American community in the United States, and some of the largest population of Gullahs are in Beaufort, SC.

Beaufort has served as the backdrop for films such as Forrest Gump.


Savannah sits by the Savannah River about 18 miles from the Atlantic Sea, amid Lowcountry swamps and mammoth live oak trees dripping with Spanish moss. It was the first settlement of the new British colony of Georgia and was founded in 1733. Savannah was a major centre for the export of cotton from the plantations based on slave labour in the 1750's.

The Deep Souths has a sub tropical climate and can reaches 41 degrees in July, thank goodness it's not that warm today. The area is also prone to hurricanes.

Agriculture was essential to Savannah's economy during its first two centuries. Silk and indigo production, both in demand in England, were early export commodities. By 1767, almost a ton of silk per year was exported to England.

Georgia's mild climate also offered perfect conditions for growing cotton, which became the dominant commodity after the American Revolution.
