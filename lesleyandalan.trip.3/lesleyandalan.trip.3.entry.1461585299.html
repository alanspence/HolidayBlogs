Blowing Rock, North Carolina, United States
Monday, April 25, 2016

Left Salem in Virginia behind and entered our third state, North Carolina to enter the Blue Ridge Parkway scenic drive which traverse the southern Appalachian Mountains. The parkway drive was commissioned by Roosevelt as a depression-era project.

Stopped to do two short trails, one to a panoramic view point towards the east of the mixed hardwood trees stretching as far as you can see. Trees are only occasionally interspersed with cleared small holdings, the other walk to a waterfall. It's the region of Bluegrass music but it's not too evident yet as the main tourist season hasn't yet started and Infacts peaks in the Autumn when the tree colours are spectacular.

Interesting the Cherokee came to these mountains to hunt, followed later by Scots/ Irish immigrants looking for a better life. Stopped the night at a motel at Blowing Rock, a high cliff from which light objects thrown over the side blow back to you.
