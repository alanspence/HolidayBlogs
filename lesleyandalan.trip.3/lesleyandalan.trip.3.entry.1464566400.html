Yosemite Valley, California, United States
Tuesday, May 31, 2016

Our route has been determined by the late snow storms in the Sierra's. The road closure of the Tiago Pass which runs from east to west across the Sierra Nevada mountains has meant we could only finalise our route at the last minute. Our knowledgeable USA family who have a home just north of here in Tahoe, had highlighted this possibility in February but we didn't expect new snow so late probably because it seems such a remote idea in the UK even in our planning days in February. The pass climbs to 9945' and has in recent years been closed to the 4th July because of snow and ice which make the road impassable.

Luckily for the last 48 hours it has been open, but as there was a 20% chance of overnight rain/ snow, it wasn't until we checked with our hotel that we were sure we could take this route. It's easy to see why it's been closed with piled up cleared snow alongside the road, and melting snow water flowing across the road. With low night time temperatures it was easy to see why the warning signs "Ice" at various places were needed although the road was ice free for us.

So, we drove along the Tioga Pass via Tuolumne Meadows, Sierras highest alpine meadow at 8600' which was very pretty. It was our last drive to another hotel, and another fantastic, beautiful one, going up high with very pretty lakes, one frozen over still. Once on the other side of the mountain range, we finally reached Yosemite valley which has dramatic scenery, the valley being cut by the River Merced giving life to the meadows surrounding it.

The valley is only seven by less than one mile wide but the 3000' feet near vertical cliffs are still as amazing as when we saw them 17 years ago.

We wandered along the Merced River for a late afternoon stroll, enjoying watching the rapidly flowing river with youngsters jumping off the bridge, canoeists and other boaters, and admiring the power of Yosemite Falls which looked magnificent with a huge volume of water.
