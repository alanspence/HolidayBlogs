Springdale, Utah, United States
Tuesday, May 24, 2016

The road from the east to Zion NP has six tight switchback turns and has two tunnels, one 1.1 miles long and one way if coaches are using it so queues develop quickly. We were lucky today no waiting. Caught the shuttle into the park as cars are prohibited at this time of year to try and preserve the area. Followed the trail on the riverside walk at the head of the valley, another to Emerald Pool and several others. Beautiful day but a slight chill in the air to start with but quickly warmed up.

Zion NP has a river running along the main canyon so it has a very different feel to the National Parks we visited over the last few days. There is lots of lush vegetation making it all look pretty and there were more flowers and a swamp area. This is still in the middle of a desert though, so there is plenty of red sand and cacti.

We saw lots of people going canyoning - most with rented gear which is waterproof over trousers like sailing trousers going up to chest height, waterproof neoprene socks, and special shoes designed for walking the canyon and being filled with water. There are warnings about flash floods all over the place and 7 people in one group died here last year. We just walked up to the first point they have to walk up the river which was flowing fast and fairly deep.

There were also warning signs on one of the trails - "people have died falling off this trail - be careful" with a warning triangle showing a body falling! We didn't actually take that path today.

There were too many people in the Park - it's the first time it's felt overcrowded in the parks - but it's a beautiful place well worth visiting and we had another lovely day.

Top of valley reached by wading
You can hire full wet suits and shoes to wade for four hours each way to head of valley. First day it was open to do this today. Season starts later than we expected. Plenty of people equipped with gear and poles to test water depth. Others less equipped in shorts and trainers participating. Seven people died last year in a flash flood doing it!
