Nashville, Tennessee, United States
Tuesday, May 3, 2016

Drove north west and leaving the ' hurricane emergency evacuation route' behind we headed to Music City and across the first time zone on our travels in the USA. We were surprised to be driving in the mountains once again as we thought we had left the vast expanse of woods of the east coast behind but we again found them near Chattanooga. This area is close to the Smokey Mountains we visited before our detour south to the Atlantic. You may know the song Chattanooga Choo Choo, well the older ones of us may? http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?lyrics=24116

Nashville was settled on Christmas Day in 1779, and is known the world over as the birthplace of Country Music. Country music fans and songwriters head to Nashville following their dreams.

We walked to 'Music Row' a short distance from the hotel, passing the studios and now offices of Warner, Sony, RCA Studio B where Elvis recorded over 200 songs, Dolly Parton, Chet Atkins and Jim Reeves recorded hits and many more.

You may notice we include random facts and photos, these are included for individual friends and family as we know they have a specific interest or connection to them but for the rest of you seem rather odd!

Continuing downtown we visited Music City Centre which is the most enormous convention centre next to the Country Music Hall of Fame which we are seeing tomorrow. There is a Cash and Dylan exhibition on at the moment.

Stopped at Roberts bar to have a beer and soak up the atmosphere of the live country music, not quite believing we are really here.

This evening we went to a live performance at the music phenomenon of the world-famous 4,400-seat Grand Ole Spry. The Grand Ole Opry is the world's longest-running radio show opened in 1925 and showcases the best country and bluegrass music. We were rather unsure what this experience would be like but it turned out to be excellent performance with the musicians engaging the audience well and more importantly the music and singing was of a high standard from Old Crow Medicine Show, Rascall Flatts, Terri Clark, Chris Janson, Locash and Larry Gatlin. We booked the tickets in February and you take pot luck with who will be appearing.
