Houston, Texas, United States
Friday, May 13, 2016

NASA has been controlling space flights here since 1965 and in fact the first word spoken in space was 'Houston'. The launches of course take place at Kennedy in Florida.

The Johnson Space Center (JSC) in Houston is the home to mission control, astronaut training, and research - it's the "brains" of the USA's manned space program. We saw where astronauts train for missions in the Mockup area. I thought these were going to be imitations but not all. The tram tours took us onto the campus to see these working areas, while in the visitor centre - called Space Centre Houston - we saw films, talks, museum pieces, touched lunar rock, and got on board a mightily impressive full-scale shuttle replica mounted on top of the original shuttle carrier aircraft NASA 905 and standing eight stories tall.

JSC is the nerve centre for the ISS which was mentioned many times as well as Tim Peake.
If you want to see where it is overhead you can go to spotthestation.nasa.gov. They see 19 sunrises and sunsets a day!
