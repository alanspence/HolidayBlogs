Second day in Washington, DC

Washington DC, District of Columbia, United States
Friday, April 22, 2016

We started early to fit in a visit to the White House exhibition - very interesting with some good graphic displays of the rooms, plus descriptions of how the White House developed. It also gave a great account of what it was like on the day one President moves out and the next moves in.

We used the Metro to get from there to the Pentagon where we went on a pre booked tour. This was led by a marine who had worked in the inner circle of trusted people around President Obama, who apparently is very good to his entourage. The marine did the tour walking backwards the whole two miles which means we had non-stop commentary. He admitted he didn't join the marines to do walking backward tours for visitors! The parts around where the terrorists flew the plane into the Pentagon was very moving, with displays of art by children, a very solemn area like a chapel, and lots about the individuals killed. We walked on to the Memorial which is open to the public at all times.

We took the Metro back to the National Gallery of Art which we went round completely, but at speed in some rooms. There were some wonderful paintings which I knew from books but was delighted to see in the flesh - the colours are always so much more vibrant and convincing. It's an amazing collection which is worth a visit to Washington to see without anything else.

Then we just about managed to overcome exhaustion to visit the "space" museum - which really has as much about aeronautics as space. It's all amazing. Our time was limited but we looked at the Apollo exhibits and the Wright brothers development of the first flights - first gliders, then with power, and how they learned what would work and what wouldn't. We finished with a Planetarium show - more amazement. Very comfortable leaning back chairs were sleep inducing but we both managed to keep awake... just.

Then we walked on to Capitol Hill and took in the views, before walking back to our hotel. We planned a route that turned out to be one block further out than perhaps we should have gone, and there the seedier side of Washington was back in our faces with the very poor hanging around on the streets. There were two tents on the sidewalk right by an ordinary street, obviously home to some poor people who could do no better. I asked out loud how could this be so within 10 minutes walk of the Capitol, the foundation of the USA?

After moving back to a busier thoroughfare and feeling safer again, we passed through China Town and stopped for a Chinese - and very welcome it was too after a second day with lots of walking. We were getting beyond tired.

What a great time we have had in Washington - it would of course be possible to spend much, much longer here, but we are crossing America and that goal means short stops, like a Chef's menu with many small courses.

What I saw

White House Visitors Center - 1450 Pennsylvania Ave. NW, Washington DC, District of Columbia, United States
I rated this place 4/5 - First stop
Good history of the White House. In 1880 Queen Victoria presented President Hayes with the Resolute desk made from timbers from HMS Resolute which has been used by many Presidents. Three tons of jelly beans were ordered for Reagan's inauguration festivities in 1981. Raegan ate jelly beans during cabinet meetings and even had a special holder for them on Air Force One so they didn't fall out in turbulence.
Capitol Hill - Washington DC, District of Columbia, United States
I rated this place 4/5 - Congress
Since 1800 it has been the legislative branch of the American Governmentshere laws are written.
National Air and Space Museum - Sixth and Independence Ave., SW, Washington DC, District of Columbia, United States
I rated this place 4/5 - Museum and Planetarium
The Wright brothers were bike shop owners who developed world changing technology from their experimental knowledge of bikes. They flew a series of successful aircraft and pioneered the practice of aeronautical engineering. The first flight was on 17 th December 1903. Amazing presentation on stars in the Planetarium.
National Gallery of Art - West Building, Constitution Avenue and 6th AvenueWashington DC, District of Columbia, United States
I rated this place 4/5 - Middle Ages to the present
Funny to see Constables Wivenhoe House and several Flatford Mill area paintings when so far from home. Picasso, Degas, Van Gogh, Matisse to name a few others.
The Pentagon - 1400 Defense Pentagon, Arlington, Virginia, United States
I rated this place 4/5 - Tour of the Pentagon
The Pentagon is the largest office building in the world. Built in 16 months for the HQ of the Department of Defence. Outside is the Pentagon memorial where 184 illuminated benches honour each person killed on 11/9/2001 in the terrorist attack. The plane crashed into the west side of the building killing all on board and over a hundred Pentagon employees.
