Vicksburg, Mississippi, United States
Saturday, May 7, 2016

Drove south out of Memphis and quickly entered the state of Mississippi, heading towards New Orleans which we reach in three days. Drivers overtake on both sides of the carriageway which takes you by surprise sometimes. Cars don't have reg plates on the front and the back ones are highly decorated.

On the way we stopped at the Vicksburg National Military Park, first watching an explanatory film about the war at the Visitor Centre and then there's a 16 mile drive of the battle sights. Brilliantly done as a memorial to both the Union and Confederate soldiers who died in the American Civil War but also showing the visitor historic markers explaining battle scenarios and key events from Vicksburg's 1863 47 day siege. The seizure on the 4th July of the access to the Mississippi by the Union soldiers was one of the turning points in the Civil War. For 81 years after this Vicksburg refused to celebrate the 4th July.

Vicksburg is on a high bluff overlooking a canalised Yazoo River as the Mississippi River's course changed since the 1860's and is a little further south. On Main Street on the top of the bluff is found the Biedenham Coca Cola Museum where Coke was first bottled anywhere in the world. With the amount of fizzy drinks consumed in the USA it's surprising it wasn't larger and didn't have a shop attached. On every street corner people are seen drinking huge containers of fizzy drinks and every restaurant gives free refills.

One thing about the deep South we continually notice is the friendliness of the people, whether it's in shops, restaurants or just going along the street. The U.K. could learn a lot about manners and customer service from these people although we are reliably told that this will get worse the further west we travel.

Dinner was a platter of broiled or blackened oysters (good thing you weren't eating A), shrimps, scallops and other fish and desert of Mississippi Mud Pie at Rusty's. Yummy. We didn't really need the Mud Pie but it seemed only right to eat one as we're at the Mississippi!
