Charleston, South Carolina, United States
Thursday, April 28, 2016

So while I love birds I have now decided that there's one species I find extremely irritating, the northern mockingbird. We had one living in a tree outside our Asheville hotel room for the last few nights. These birds sing at night, yes that's right night. The USA family find our ignorance amusing and their response was to eradicate it. Good idea we just don't have the right equipment.

Apparently northern mockingbirds may have a repertoire of over 200 different songs and I believe it. During their long nocturnal serenades, they are capable of singing over 1,000 songs per hour and trust me they do, it eventually quietened  at 3 am!! This is their normal behaviour.

First long drive of four hours today. Driving here is such a pleasure, interstates are well maintained and easy to drive on. Roadworks mean you drop your speed by 5-10 mph and don't have to go at 30-40 for 20 miles like in the UK. Food outlets even if they are mostly chips and burgers are frequent and petrol garages are well signed when you take the exit.

We arrived in Charleston on the coast  by 14.00 so took the free tram downtown to the harbour area. Docked there was a huge cruise ship from London which was on a 5 week tour of the Carribean. Nice to see the Union Jack! 

Weather today was hot with temperatures reaching 33 degrees but that brought a short rain shower at 5 pm. As expected that cleared the air beautifully.
