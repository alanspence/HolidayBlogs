New Orleans, Louisiana, United States
Wednesday, May 11, 2016

Didn't take the streetcar to the centre today but did a casual stroll along Magazine Street. It is a six-mile road which we joined at the Garden District where we saw the beautiful houses yesterday and walked in to Uptown. The shopping road includes some of the best antique stores, art galleries, craft shops and boutiques in the city. Shame Virgin have a weight allowance.

We returned to the Caribbean-colonial architecture of the French Quarter, this time armed with our city walking tour route. This idea always produced a groan with our kids when they were younger although they have been known in their old age to even get a book out of the library, while others just buy of the holiday area they are about to visit. What turns around, comes around!

The Presbytere, designed for the rectory for the nearby St Louis Cathedral acts as the Mardi Gras museum. Masks and plastic beads adorn street corners and trees still from the festival in March.

We stopped at a jazz bar and saw a great band there and there were also numerous spontaneous acts playing music in the street. We relaxed in the afternoon sun. 29 degrees today but the humidity made it feel much hotter.

Tomorrow is a driving day, our first big drive as we push west to Houston and towards our family in San Francisco.
