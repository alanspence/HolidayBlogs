Memphis, Tennessee, United States
Friday, May 6, 2016

Well when staying in Memphis it goes without saying you have to visit Graceland, his colonial style mansion which he bought for $100,000 aged 22 years old. The self guided individual iPad tour of the house where Elvis lived for 20 years lets you wander at your own pace. The tour takes you to the downstairs rooms only, in respect to him as he died in an upstairs bathroom and into various areas like his racket court, office, pool and where he is now buried in the garden.

Yesterday saw the 20 millionth visitor to Graceland, a woman from London, so close, we could have been speaking to Lisa Presley on the phone as our prize!

The two planes Elvis owned made an interesting addition to the visit and the luxury he flew in was impressive. These are due to be moved later this year and visitors will be required to pay an additional fee at the new site to see them.

Elvis was born in Mississippi and was inspired by the blue clubs of Beale Street which we visited later in the day. Drinks served in 'fish bowls' were a popular feature. Live bands entertain the tourists along both sides of the road.

We saw the upstairs balcony where Martin Luther King was shot in 1968 at the 1950's style Lorraine Motel which is now part of the National Civil Rights Museum and caught the tram back to our car as we were worn out, holidays are tiring but so much fun!
