Garden District and Swamp

New Orleans, Louisiana, United States
Tuesday, May 10, 2016

The Garden District with impressive mansions and smaller shotgun houses with shady tree lined streets provided a lovely place to stroll in the 85 degree heat this morning. Saw the house where Edward Degas lived in a 1852 Italianate house. Degas always reminds us of Cat as she studied his ballet paintings at Philip Morant and had pictures in her bedroom wall, unfortunately not originals.

We drove north east to the Honey Swamp in the afternoon to do a highly recommended swamp tour. We weren't disappointed. Situated off the Pearl River named because of pearls sometimes forming in the clams found here, the Honey Swamp gets its name as bees nest in the hollow cypress trees leaving honey behind.

The guides feed the alligators marshmallows to encourage them to come to the boats. Apparently there is estimated to be 2 million alligators in Louisiana. We saw a huge heron and an egret flying through the swamp, snakes, alligators, a hog, birds and a hawk in its nest feeding three young high in the trees. Owls, nutrias (large tree rats) and deer live in the area too but we didn't catch sight of them today. The scenery in the swamp is breathtaking. Along the river there are lots of ramshackle homes and places used as a base for hunting and fishing expeditions. There were also some rather smart houses only accessible by boat. The swamp boat was powered by a 200hp four stroke engine which moved us along the main river very well, but was also very maneuverable in the tight spaces between submerged tree stumps in the narrow waterways in the swamp. The swamp tour was a thrilling thing to do.
